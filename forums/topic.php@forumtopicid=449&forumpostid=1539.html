As far as I can tell, the reason this is happening is the AltGr store includes áéíóú which are mapped in the UK keyboard on Ctrl+Alt+A,E,I,O and U respectively. In order to match other letters, include [RALT 'n'] in the AltGr store (I have tested this and it worked).

This then causes the following rule to fire:
If(GiMFlag = 'Off') + Any(AllGlyphs) > Use(MuM)

Also: because the RAlt (or Ctrl+Alt) AEIOU keys on a UK keyboard generate áéíóú characters on the UK keyboard, they will also fire on nomatch rules.
