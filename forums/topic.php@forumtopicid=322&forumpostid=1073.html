# RE: How can I type open o for example?   2011-12-13 10:17:28.900
Peirce Baehr
Good news — the "ɔ" (open o) is available on the Moore keyboard, by pressing:
AltGr o

If you don't have an AltGr key on your computer (to the right of the SHIFT), in the Options tab of Keyman Configuration tick 'Simulate AltGr with Ctrl+Alt' and click ‹OK›. Then, you can type "ɔ" (open o) by pressing:
Ctrl Alt o

You can also use AltGr or Ctrl+Alt to type a long list of other characters, including ɖ, ŋ, ɦ, and ɟ.
